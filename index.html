Enabling Interactive Virtual Reality for Room Acoustics in Enclosed Spaced Using Reduced Basis Methods

 

We present a proof-of-concept of a virtual reality simulation tool based on the reduced basis method (RBM) for the low frequencies in combination with a geometrical acoustic method for the high frequencies. An actual meeting room located at Ramb.ll Denmark offices is modelled in 3D, to emulate an ongoing online meeting. Interactivity enables to change between different acoustic conditions and two receiver positions. Head orientation is enabled by means of real-time binaural rendering using second-order ambisonics. The reduced order model (ROM) is constructed by parameterizing the flow resistivity of the porous material at the surfaces, achieving speedups by a factor of 420 without introducing audible differences. This concept prototype proves the potential of RBM in building design and interactive virtual auditory displays.
